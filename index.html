The most beautiful nature sites I have visited in Israel

Our country is a beautiful place and there are breathtaking places. In the southern region there are amazing mountain routes and in the north region there are streams and waterfalls that make you want to move there.

THE LOST LAKE

What makes the place unique beyond its discovery is the impressive contrast between the water in blue and turquoise tones and the red cliffs that surround it, which together create a stunning landscape in the middle of the desert. The source of the lake water is actually groundwater that rises and floods the copper quarries in the area.

Nahal El Al

The phenomenon of a white waterfall and a black waterfall is unique and fascinating. The basalt stones of the Golan cover thick layers of white marine sedimentary rocks. The water goes under the black basalt rock and creates a waterfall in it and then continues and goes deeper until it reaches the brick rocks and this time creates a waterfall in the sedimentary rocks under the basalt.

The Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is divided into two basins a deep northern basin and a shallow southern basin. In the last twenty years the level of the Dead Sea has dropped twelve meters and the southern basin is dry. A feed canal currently flows water from the northern basin to the southern basin. The southern basin is covered with evaporation ponds, belonging to the Dead Sea factories.

Nahal Snir Hatsbani

Nahal Snir begins its course on the western slopes of Mount Hermon starting at an altitude of about 1,550 m above sea level. In the area of ​​the Lebanese town of Hatzabia, Nahal Snir receives water throughout the year from the Hatzabia springs. The town of Hatzabia and the Hatzabia springs gave the stream its name, Hatzabani, in Arabic. From the Hatzabia springs and to the south, constant and fresh water flows in a stream all year round.
